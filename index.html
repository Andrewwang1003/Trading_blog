Standard Breakout Strategy

Definition: A breakout occurs when a stock price moves above a key resistance level with increased volume, signaling potential continuation of the upward trend. As Quallamaggie defines it: "A breakout is when a stock breaks above a previous high or resistance level with volume, creating a new higher high and establishing a new uptrend. The key is clean consolidation before the breakout - the tighter the base, the better the breakout."

Key Components:

  • Price breaks above a clear resistance level (previous highs, trend lines, or consolidation patterns)
  • Volume increases significantly (at least 150% of average daily volume)
  • Clean consolidation pattern before the breakout
  • Strong relative strength compared to the market

Entry Criteria:

  • Enter on the breakout candle or retest of the breakout level
  • Stop loss at low of day or 1.5 ADR below entry
  • Target: Until a close under the 10 EMA

Episodic Pivot Strategy

Definition: An episodic pivot is a reversal pattern that occurs after a significant move, where the stock finds support and begins a new upward trend. Popularized by Quallamaggie, this strategy focuses on catching the early stages of new momentum moves.

Key Components:

  • Stock has had a significant move down or sideways consolidation
  • Volume dries up near the lows, indicating selling exhaustion
  • Price begins to stabilize and form higher lows
  • Volume increases on the first significant up day
  • Stock shows relative strength vs. market

Entry Criteria:

  • Enter on the first strong volume day after the pivot
  • Stop loss below the pivot low or recent swing low
  • Target: Previous resistance levels or 2-3x risk

Example Patterns:

BE (7/24/25): Bloom Energy episodic pivot after earnings selloff, found support and began new uptrend
PGEN (8/15/25): Precigen pivot on biotech sector recovery, volume dried up then increased on reversal
PL (9/18/25): Planet Labs episodic pivot after space sector correction, clean reversal pattern
