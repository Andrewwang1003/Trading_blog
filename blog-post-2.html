Slow Progress and Patience

September 23, 2025

It has been a while since I last checked in, but my positions have been slowly grinding up, and I am starting to see real progress again. I have learned that the key to making money is not constant trading, but giving my positions time to work. The longer I hold quality setups, the more clearly I can see how patience pays off.

I used to think that trading more meant improving faster, but now I understand that taking fewer, higher-quality trades is what really moves the needle. I am almost back to break-even and feeling more comfortable in the markets than I have in a long time. I am beginning to take on slightly more risk when the opportunity is right, but it feels controlled and calculated rather than impulsive.

Positions like BE, NUGT, TEM, and KTOS have been carrying my account, each one slowly pushing my portfolio higher. Watching them grind upward day after day reinforces what I have been trying to learn for months—discipline and patience always win over overtrading.

All side projects, including the superperformance analysis, are paused for now as college applications take priority. Even with everything going on, I still feel focused on trading and confident that I am finally building the habits that will carry me long-term. Slow progress is still progress, and I am learning to trust that.
