Trusting Stop Losses

June 30, 2025

Recently, my trades have been alright. All the positions I took were technically sound and had a decent conviction; however, they lacked significant momentum behind them. There is a possibility I'm trying to chase the dream set up, the ones I have been watching and keeping track of. The ones I have been missing and wishing I had gotten into.

Because of this constant chase, I might be overtrading on chasing that setup, and instead, not focusing on the solid ones. IONQ, NBIS, RDDT, and MSTR were good trades today. Although there wasn't any strong follow-through on NBIS, it looked promising. The rest had follow-through but stopped me out on the way, or didn't have a picture perfect entry.

I think that I should take stuff only based on a 5-minute high to raise my conviction and limit the amount of risk I would be taking. That would have stopped me from taking NBIS. I just got stopped out of RDDT, and then it started pushing higher; there's not much I can do about that. IONQ had a weak opening, resulting in a reasonably high risk compared to the reward. I'm keeping IONQ as I like the name, and it has a good history of follow-through.

One thing that I'm very frustrated with is MSTR. I took this off a 5-minute high retake, which started strong but was extremely choppy. After I was up a little bit, it faded back into my entry, and all my positions seemed weak at the time, so I just decided to get rid of the position. However, afterward, it pushed higher than before, pushing 1RR above my entry, causing me to miss out on some serious gains.

At least I didn't lose the positions, which could have been a very realistic outcome. This is another reminder for me to follow my trading rules and stick to my stop-losses and take-profits. There is no reason to overdo it and try different strategies in hopes of avoiding a slight loss.

Overall, I realized from this trading day to keep myself in check and trust my stop losses. Ideally, after 7:30, I should be off my computer and minding my own business, and check back into my positions before the market closes. When I spend too much time on my laptop, I tend to overthink and end up doing something I regret. I just need to stay calm and relax.

My conviction today was good, but the market-moving money just wasn't there, and that's okay. Tomorrow will be another trading day with a decent amount of funds, so I should be able to take some good-sized and leveraged positions. It only takes me 2-3 good trades with 1:15 RR for me to come back and make up for all my losses.

Keep in mind I have only been trading with real money for a month and have been trading for only 7 months, so I have a lot of time to learn and perfect my strategy. No one said trading would be easy, as it's a tough and extremely mentally challenging endeavor. It's okay, though. I'm building a skill and starting early, which I'll have 5 years to practice and work on before I enter the real world. I have time, and I promise to be profitable. Perfection takes time, and that's all I have.
